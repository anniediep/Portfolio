For a variety of reasons, people are choosing to live in smaller more efficient spaces, often renting over buying a home, which means they have less space to store things. They wish they could rent instead of buying items they have limited use for. The challenge was to create a product that would foster a sense of community and ease that comes with borrowing items from a neighbor.

We started this project from scratch and dived into research to understand if there was a space and a need for peer to peer rental. In particular, we looked into the concept of sharing and concerns around borrowing items. Two key insights bubbled up and became the root of our product: users want full transparency and safety measures when receiving and returning rented items from strangers. Through user testing, we created the rental process where users can rent items from people in their community and coordinate delivery with ease.

Process

Renting from people in your community

The features in Trellis are meant to enhance one's ability to rent items that weren't previously accessible to them and allow them to connect with others in their community in a safe and transparent manner.

RENTAL DISTANCE

Trellis users can identify approximately how far the item they want to rent is from them on the product page.

CHAT WITH OWNER

Users can figure out if the item they want fit's their expectations and get their questions answered.

RENTAL TRANSPARENCY

Trellis showcases what users can expect when they decide to rent with transparent pricing and outlined expectations.

ITEM RATING

Rental items will be rated individually so users know what to expect when they rent.

COORDINATED DELIVERY

Users can coordinate delivery and returns through Messenger or opt to use a delivery service.

Designing for Transparency

In our research, we found that many of the concerns related to a peer-to-peer rental platform was transparency in the rental process. For this reason, transparency was our main consideration while designing Trellis. This helped with three features: rental categories, pricing and conditions, and choosing to show item rating over owner rating.

Conclusion

Trellis taps into the share and gig economy to allow users in a community to connect and rent items from each other. More than that it provides a safe and transparent way to do so. Our team focused on one user flow within the allotted time frame. If we had more time we would have liked to further explore the categorization of products, especially clothing and luxury items.
