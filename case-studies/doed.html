The U.S. Department of Education Website Redesign

The U.S. Department of Education (DoED) is an organization whose mission is to promote student achievement and preparation for global competitiveness. In this personal project, I redesign their website to match the mental model of a student.

TEAM

Annie Diep - Personal Project

TOOLS

Miro, Illustrator, Adobe XD, Trello, and G Suite

PROBLEM

The DoED site as-is has a ton of information but no centralized focus, making navigation difficult for a student looking to lower the cost of their tuition.

SOLUTION

I did this by creating a roadmap, restructuring the content and messaging of the site so that navigation is clear and the content doesn’t overwhelm the user. Additionally, I updated the branding to reflect the organization.

RESEARCH

I learned that students are overwhelmed while searching for grants and scholarships on the DoED’s website. From the data collected from my user interviews and accessibility, these are the insights I gathered.

From my own research I could understand why students were frustrated with finding that the grants that applied to them. There's a lack of focus and way too much information.

Additionally, I looked into accessibility. It’s even more crucial since the DoED’s mission is to give equal access to students.

DEFINITION

In the next step of the design process, I created documents that help keep me aligned to the goals set with consideration to the insights collected. One item being the user persona, which helped me empathize with the user.

Card Sorting was necessary to sort through all the information housed on DoED. It helped give me clarity in organizing the contents of the site.

Helping a user navigate to finding funding for their tuition through a user flow helped me focus on the task at hand. I chose to focus on applying for federal grants.

ITERATION

I put some wire frames together and conducted a 5 second test. These quick test helped identify mental disconnects with a student. The biggest changes were made in the dropdown, sub-level navigation, and highlighting the Pell Grant.

Style Guide

Colors were pulled from the logo to create a simple color pallet for the style guide. I wanted to keep screens clean and have images that convey proper imagery that's serious yet playful.

Conclusion & Next Steps

  • Users liked my design and said they would use this website.
  • I was surprised by the confusion on what is clickable and what isn't. It's humbling when I thought it was obvious but I was too close to the product.
  • It's significantly faster to design if you work from the style guide.
  • I think the next difficult but necessary area to design is the Student Loans page. It would be interesting to see how to best integrate a login from an external website.
