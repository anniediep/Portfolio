Front Steps Website Redesign

Front Steps is a non-profit in Austin, TX. It focuses on solving homelessness in the greater Austin area. In this project, our team designed a resource roadmap for a person experiencing homelessness.

TEAM

Annie Diep, Sebastian Elias, and Judy Nguyen

TOOLS

Miro, Illustrator, Adobe XD, Trello, and G Suite

Scope

2 weeks

MY DELIVERABLES

User and Stakeholder Interviews, User Persona, Donor Survey, Empathy Map, Sketches, Wireframes, A/B Testing, Style Guide, and Desktop Hi-Fi Prototypes.

PROBLEM

One of the most effective ways of solving homelessness is through prevention. Front Steps' prioritizes solving homelessness by equipping those experiencing it with resources. Yet, their target users are not utilizing the website as a resource to do so.

SOLUTION

Our team created a roadmap to help guide people to the resources they need immediately.

DEFINITION

We synthesized our data and created a user persona to better empathize with the user.

Homelessness is best solved through prevention, so we focused on getting people to the resources they need in our user task flow. In a website context, the most effective way we can help is by improving SEO.

ITERATION

Sketches

We referred to other non-profit sites as inspiration before sketching. Enclosures and proximity were key to showcase related information here. We ended up with two homepages, an immediate help page, and a job training page.

Branding

Before transitioning to hi-fi, I created a style guide. I used a minimal color pallet to keep things simple and clean as requested by our stakeholders. Additionally, I selected respectful imagery that's symbolic of the context.

Testing & Improvements

In the next iteration, guerilla testing helped us understand where the gaps were.

We had one more round of user tests and wanted to get a representative user. Luckily, we found someone at the downtown library. He provided the most valuable feedback by shedding light on our user’s mental model.

REFLECTION

Conclusion & Next Steps

  • Our users expressed interest and excitement for our redesign because it addresses the daily struggles they deal with.
  • What’s not showcased here is scope creep. We designed more screens in hopes of helping Front Steps realize their vision.
  • This was a difficult project because there are a lot of unknowns with homelessness. A lot of footwork and research were necessary to understand a mental model that’s not as well understood.
  • The next logical step is to see if there's a need for a visual time table to address our users' daily cognitive load.
