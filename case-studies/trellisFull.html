A Peer-to-Peer Rental Platform

Trellis is a web application that brings communities together through the sharing economy. Rent items from your community that prevents you from accomplishing your goals. Trellis helps people get access to items that were previously unreachable.

PROBLEM

For a variety of reasons, people are choosing to live in smaller more efficient spaces, often renting over buying a home, which means they have less space to store things. They wish they could rent instead of buying items that they have limited use for.

SOLUTION

We created a peer-to-peer rental platform that encourages community engagement and eco-friendly practices. Additional focus was placed on ease of use for searching and renting items.

RESEARCH

We interviewed 5 users, 1 business, and sent out a survey. The goal was to learn more about the concept of renting over buying.

Although we had direct competitors, we found that this product isn’t currently offered in the US.

DEFINITION

In order to bring this idea to life, we created a user persona. One that aligns with our interviews and insights.

When you can rent anything from a community, what does that mean? We explored categorizing different items to cover our categories wholistically. Two items, in particular, gave us the most headaches: a ladder and a stroller.

We chose to work on the rental process from the renter’s perspective. The photo below shows us working through categories and figuring out how to make the search process easier. While the diagram on the right is the simplified flow.

ITERATION

Sketches and Wireframes

Sketching was a very smooth process with most of the discussion revolving around the search bar placement and how our users will filter items.

Style Guide

Our team thought it was important to create a simple, clean design. Even more so because Trellis will be full of photos of all kinds of rental items. We chose green to touch on the environmental friendly aspect of Trellis.

Testing & Improvements

After creating our wireframes we tested our user flow. Users had no issues completing the task. However, there were some concerns raised that we addressed below.

We were curious about whether people would impulse rent items. And debated the importance of transparency and maximizing above-the-fold content.

Final Thoughts

  • This project was a success. We identified a gap in the U.S. market that hasn’t been tapped yet. Trellis supports a circular economy of reusing resources. From a business perspective, there's little to maintain outside of logistics. Overall, a great return in business value that has buy-in from both rentees and renters.
  • This was a great team to work with. We had some healthy internal debates about aspects of our design and made good use of whiteboards, windows, and sticky notes to work through our design challenges.
  • We found ourselves with many parking lot items. The next logical step is to look into improving the categorization of products, especially clothing and luxury items.
  • Given more time, I would build out the last responsive page in the flow.
